How does it work?

Our system allows you to measure the velocity and accuracy of a dodgeball throw by analyzing video recordings from two different camera angles: one on the side and one underneath the throwing area. These cameras can be mobile cameras, making the setup process simple and accessible.

Camera Setup

Follow these steps to set up your mobile cameras:

  1. Position one camera on the side of the throwing area, ensuring it captures only the end of the ball's trajectory before it reaches the target (such as a cross).
  2. Position the second camera underneath the throwing area, also capturing only the end of the ball's trajectory before it reaches the target.
  3. Adjust the height and angle of both cameras to ensure they capture the best possible view of the throw's final moments. This will allow the system to accurately measure velocity and accuracy.
  4. Ensure both cameras are securely mounted and won't move during the recording process.

Once your cameras are set up, you can record the dodgeball throw and upload the videos to our platform. The system will analyze the recordings, calculate the velocity and accuracy of the throw, and display the results.

Measuring Results

After uploading your videos, our system will calculate the following metrics:

  • Velocity: The speed of the dodgeball throw, measured in kilometers per hour (km/h).
  • Accuracy: The distance from the center of the target, measured in centimeters (cm).

With these results, you can track your performance over time, make adjustments to your throwing technique, and track your progress in improving your dodgeball skills.
